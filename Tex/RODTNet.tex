\chapter{基于拓扑约束下不确定性驱动特征调弈的锂离子电池分布外鲁棒退化建模方法}\label{chap:RODTNet}

\section{引言}\label{sec:RODTNet_introduction}

在电动汽车作为可持续交通体系载体的关键性越发显现的背景下，其动力总成中的储能部件，LIBs，的重要性不言而喻\cite{10766661, 9849057, 10897846, WASEEM2025236414}。
LIBs 不断进行充放电循环并在这一过程中发生性能退化。
导致可用续航里程削减，热失控风险增加，退役后梯次利用的经济价值降低\cite{LI2024155588}。
从而，对 LIBs 的性能退化建模重要且迫切。通常使用一对各自有所侧重的指标系统评估 LIBs 的性能退化程度，分别是 RUL 和 SOH。
具体地，前者通过量化容量衰减至寿命终止阈值前的剩余循环次数来提供前瞻性预测，后者则通过当前容量与标称容量的比值来反映当前状态。

% para 2
鉴于车载应用对非侵入式测量的限制，LIBs 的 RUL 和 SOH 只能依据车载电池管理系统（Vehiclar Battery Management Systems, VBMS）采集的监测数据，通过构建退化模型进行间接预测或估计。相比于难以应对复杂多物理场耦合效应的传统机理方法，数据驱动方法，尤其是基于深度学习的方法，在建立循环信号与退化状态之间的非线性映射方面展现出显著优势 \cite{XIAO2023107347, XIE2024630}。然而，此类方法的预测过估计性能高度依赖训练数据与测试数据同分布的前置假设，这种假设在复杂多变的实际驾驶工况下往往难以成立。此外，获取覆盖全运行工况的高质量带标签数据伴随着高昂的成本，这进一步加剧了数据驱动模型在实际应用中的泛化难度。

% para 3
现有研究广泛探讨了数据驱动 LIBs 退化建模问题中的训练与测试数据分布漂移问题，并主要借助预训练微调范式或域自适应范式构建模型 \cite{10177255}。然而，此类方法高度依赖目标域数据的可访问性。在已部署的车载系统中，由于通信带宽瓶颈、隐私保护法规以及实时计算资源的约束，获取充足的目标域数据存在很大现实阻碍 \cite{10886998, 10663939, 9849057}。进一步地，LIBs 退化过程对应连续的目标空间且具有内在的序数演化特征，这使其数据分布从根本上区别于异常检测与故障诊断等依赖离散标签的任务的数据分布 \cite{10214203, 10577994}。这种独有的序数特征导致在故障诊断等领域中已被充分研究和验证的泛化失效，仅利用源域数据实现 OOD 退化模式泛化的难度很大。尽管部分融合表征学习或元学习的策略在一定程度上实现了无需目标域数据的 LIBs 退化建模 \cite{TAN2024103725, CHEN2024234696}，但其建模过程仍依赖于预设的退化先验知识过预设的退化相关任务。与之相对地，基于数据调弈（Data Manipulation, DM）的域泛化范式 \cite{9782500} 凭借其简洁性和与底层数据驱动模型解耦的灵活性展现出优势。然而，在建立从原始传感输入到最终退化指标的多阶段复杂信息映射时，基于数据调弈的泛化策略仍需以下三个挑战：

（1）第一个挑战来自于循环数据时空信息的稀缺性，这来源于车载应用对非侵入式测量的限制。
在时间维度上，等间隔采样机制导致 VBMs 在动态复杂且对退化高度敏感的工况区间内数据采集过于稀疏 \cite{9648002, BOSE2023107543, zhu2022data, GUAN2018204, XIONG2020110048}，却在信息量较低的平稳运行阶段产生冗余记录，进而造成对关键时间退化特征的表征不足。
在空间维度上，车载循环数据仅包含有限的传感器模态，所能获取的多物理场信息受限，难以有效捕捉热力学应力 \cite{XIONG2020110048} 与机械变形 \cite{zeng2023nonintrusive} 等 LIBs 内部的关键退化机制。
如图\ref{fig:rodtnet_challenges}(a) 所示，若直接基于这样具有时空稀疏性的原始循环信号建模，将使得映射出退化特征表现复杂的高维流形结构，导致提取的退化特征缺乏必要的拓扑连续性，后续 DM 操作易使模型过拟合局部噪声，并降低其在训练分布之外的泛化性能。

% para 5
（2）第二个挑战在于未考虑域漂移在特征空间表征中引入的统计不确定性。LIBs 性能退化过程的内在复杂性及其对工况的高度敏感性 \cite{LI2024155588}，导致退化模式在多变的运行条件下不可避免地呈现出分化。这种分化直接引发了数据分布的漂移现象，使得基于源域数据训练的模型在面对分布不同的 OOD 数据时，产生统计不确定性 \cite{9577663, pmlr-v202-dai23d, liuncertainty}。如图~\ref{fig:rodtnet_challenges}(b) 所示，域漂移主要表现为由充电协议、电极材料及放电深度等差异引起的协变量漂移，以及由不同退化轨迹演变导致的概念漂移。由域漂移引起的统计不确定性破坏了模型习得的特征与退化状态之间映射关系的一致性，限制了模型向未见目标域的泛化能力，最终阻碍数据驱动方法的实际应用。

% para 6
（3）第三个挑战源于多域长尾退化分布，这一挑战本质上是由 LIBs 内部复杂的热电耦合反应导致的，在宏观上表现为高度多样化的非线性退化轨迹。
具体地，LIBs 性能退化轨迹包括不止一个阶段，其多阶段动态演化过程通常伴随明显的容量拐点，标志着从早期和中期缓慢衰减向后期加速退化的转变。
这种多阶段非线性的退化过程导致循环退化数据相对集中于 LIBs 寿命的早期和中期阶段，对应地，后期加速退化阶段的样本分布则较为稀疏。
如图~\ref{fig:rodtnet_challenges}(c) 中的快速充电工况实例所示，非线性退化特性产生了不对称的长尾样本分布。未能显式建模此分布特征的模型在面对低频次尾部退化样本时表现出严重的性能下降。
长尾分布使得直接进行 DM 操作将使得模型向数据量庞大的早中期寿命阶段倾斜，产生破坏模型跨域泛化能力的偏态特征表征。此外，长尾分布在多个不同源域之间存在异质性，这进一步增加了退化特征空间的不连续程度，阻碍了面向实际应用场景的鲁棒退化建模的实现。

% para 7
\sloppy
为应对上述挑战，本章提出一种鲁棒分布外退化模式跟踪网络（Robust Out-of-Distribution Degradation  Tracking Network, RODTNet），及其长尾优化变体（Robust Out-of-Distribution Degradation Tracking Network with Long-Tail Optimization, RODTNet-LT）。
% 
首先，为克服时空信息稀缺问题，提出基于流形收缩的鲁棒退化表征提取策略（Manifold Contraction-based Strategy for Robust Degradation Representation, MCRDR）。通过解析退化特征流形的拓扑结构，基于具备良泛化能力表征的几何约束，融合启发式信号重构策略与 Mamba 架构实现有效的可泛化特征映射。
% 
其次，为有效应对域分布漂移，提出不确定性驱动的特征合成策略（Uncertainty-Driven Feature Synthesis, UDFS）。利用多元高斯分布对目标域的固有统计规律进行量化与采样，向外拓展已学习的退化特征流形的分布边界，增强数据驱动的退化建模模型的跨域泛化的能力。
% 
最后，针对多域长尾分布，提出感知长尾分布的自适应重平衡微调策略（Long-Tail-Aware Adaptive Rebalancing Refinement, LARR）。利用高斯核平滑机制引导特征合成过程向低密度退化模式空间探索，进而在标签空间层面重塑并实现了更加均衡的特征表征。RODTNet 和 RODTNet-LT 贡献如下：

（1）提出基于流形收缩的鲁棒退化表征提取策略，约束特征流形复杂度和连续性，生成 OOD 鲁棒退化表征。

（2）提出不确定性驱动的特征合成策略，通过统计不确定性量化域变异性，指导域不变特征提取和 OOD 退化模式合成。

（3）提出感知长尾分布的自适应重平衡策略，利用特征空间与标签空间间相似性及分布平滑技术，缓解特征合成的头部偏见，增强针对不平衡退化轨迹的泛化能力。

（4）在 4 个 LIBs 老化数据集上通过 11 个 OOD 泛化任务系统验证 RODTNet 和 RODTNet-LT 的有效性，证明其相较于 14 种基线方法及其 20 种变体的先进性，阐述将其应用在可靠 VBMS 中的潜力。

\section{基于拓扑约束下不确定性驱动特征调弈的退化建模方法}\label{sec:RODTNet_methodology}

    \centering

    \bicaption{RODTNet(-LT) 架构图}{Diagram of the RODTNet-(LT) framework}
    \label{fig:rodtnet_framework}

为提升智能 VBMS 中预测算法的可靠性，并增强系统面对未见退化模式的鲁棒性，本章提出鲁棒分布外退化跟踪网络 RODTNet 及其针对长尾分布优化的变体 RODTNet-LT。如图 \ref{fig:rodtnet_framework} 所示，RODTNet 包含两个基础组件，即基于流形收缩的鲁棒退化表征提取策略 MCRDR 和不确定性驱动的特征合成方法 UDFS。在此基础上，RODTNet-LT 进一步引入感知长尾分布的自适应重平衡微调机制 LARR，以应对不平衡退化数据分布引发的泛化挑战。
% 
具体地，提取策略 MCRDR 首先利用启发式信号重构处理原始车载传感器数据，并借助基于 Mamba 架构的特征提取器将其映射为具备低拓扑复杂度和高拓扑连续性的潜在流形表征，从而克服时空信息稀疏的局限性。
% 
其次，特征合成方法 UDFS 对已知源域退化模式的特征分布进行统计建模，定量评估由域偏移引发的统计不确定性，并以此为引导合成逼近目标域真实物理分布的模拟 OOD 特征，从而增强模型的 OOD 泛化能力。
% 
最后，微调机制 LARR 通过自适应地重新校准并重平衡特征空间，进一步优化前序特征的分布结构。该机制提升网络对高频常规模式与稀有尾部退化轨迹的综合表征能力，有效解决多域长尾分布问题。

针对分布偏移条件下的 LIBs 退化建模问题，本节给出 OOD 退化建模的形式化定义。令 $\boldsymbol{x}_{t} \in \mathbb{R}^{L \times C}$ 表示 LIBs 在第 $t$ 次循环时采集的多传感器监测信号序列，其中 $L$ 为序列长度，$C$ 为传感器通道数；同时令 $y_{t} \in \mathbb{R}$ 表示对应的剩余使用寿命或健康状态等退化表征量。成对观测值 $(\boldsymbol{x}_{t}, y_{t})$ 构成定义在样本空间 $\mathcal{X} \times \mathcal{Y}$ 上的随机变量 $(X, Y)$ 的实现样本，该随机变量服从边缘分布为 $P_{X}$ 与 $P_{Y}$ 的联合概率分布 $P_{XY}$。实际应用中，汽车驾驶工况与环境温度等外部物理条件，以及 LIBs 内部电化学状态的动态演变，共同构成复杂多样的运行域。假设系统总计包含 $K = M + N$ 个域，记为 $D^{i} = \{ (\boldsymbol{x}_{t}, y_{t}) \} \sim P^{i}_{XY}$，其中 $i=1,\dots,K$。该问题的核心挑战在于不同域之间存在显著的分布偏移现象，即对于任意 $i \neq j$ 均满足 $P^{i}_{XY} \neq P^{j}_{XY}$。鲁棒 OOD 退化建模的根本目标在于，仅利用采集自 $M$ 个源域 $\mathcal{S} = \{ D^{i} \}_{i=1}^{M}$ 的数据，训练一个由参数 $\theta$ 决定的映射模型 $f_{\theta}: \mathcal{S} \to \mathcal{T}$。该模型需具备在训练阶段完全不接触目标域数据的前提下，泛化至 $N$ 个未见目标域 $\mathcal{T} = \{ D^{j} \}_{j=M+1}^{M+N}$ 的能力。在数学表达上，该目标等效于最小化模型在未见目标域上的期望预测损失 $\ell$，即求解最优化问题 $\mathop{\min}_{\theta}\mathbb{E}_{(\boldsymbol{x}, y) \in \mathcal{T}} [\ell(f_{\theta}(\boldsymbol{x}), y)]$，其中期望值严格基于目标域的真实数据分布计算。

\subsection{基于流形收缩的鲁棒退化表征提取策略}

To address the challenge of insufficient spatiotemporal coverage, we design MCRDR based on manifold contraction that emphasizes critical temporal segments and achieves spatial dimension expansion, enabling subsequent feature synthesis to further increase feature density.
First, we investigate the relationship between the generalization ability of degradation features and their manifold topology, exploring the geometric properties of well-generalized representations.
Second, we propose a heuristic cyclic signal reconstruction method possessing potentially low topological complexity.
Finally, the reconstructed input are mapped onto the feature space using a Mamba-based model.

（1）拓扑视角下的退化表征分析

Inspired by studies in relevant fields such as fault diagnosis, we investigate the geometric properties of generalizable features of degradation models.
Omitting cycle and domain indices for notational clarity, we explicitly consider the latent variable $\boldsymbol{z}$ corresponding to input $\boldsymbol{x}$. Consequently, the domain is defined as $D = \{ (\boldsymbol{x}, \boldsymbol{z}, y) \}$, adapting the source $\mathcal{S}$ and target $\mathcal{T}$ domain structure accordingly.
Let $Z$ be the random variable for $z \in \mathcal{Z}$ with distribution $P_{Z}$, we assume these representations reside on a manifold $\mathcal{M}_{Z}$ embedded within the feature space.
We assess degradation representations using the Information Bottleneck (IB) \cite{alemi2017deep} principle for quality and Intrinsic Dimension (IntDim) \cite{10.5555/3692070.3694510} for geometry.
The IB principle seeks an optimal representation $Z$ by maximizing its relevance to the label $Y$, $\mathcal{I}(Z;Y)$, while minimizing the information it retains about the input $X$, $\mathcal{I}(Z;Y)$. This objective is formulated as
\begin{equation}
    \min_{Z} \mathcal{IB} = \min_{Z} [- \mathcal{I}(Z;Y) + \beta \mathcal{I}(Z;X)], 
    \label{IB_mutual_info}
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{I}(\cdot;\cdot)$ is mutual information, and $\beta > 0$ is a Lagrangian multiplier. 
The IntDim is the minimum dimension for isometric manifold embedding.
Given the covariate shift inherent in degradation generalization, the IB objective is reformulated, focusing on the relationship between $Z$ and $Y$, formulated as
\begin{equation}
    \min_{Z} \mathcal{IB} = \min_{Z} [(1-\beta) \mathcal{H}(Y \mid Z) + \beta \mathcal{H}(Z \mid Y)], 
    \label{IB_cond_entropy}
\end{equation}
with conditional entropy $\mathcal{H}(\cdot \mid \cdot)$ and weight $\beta \in (0, 1)$.
The $\mathcal{H}(Y \mid Z)$ term encourages label predictability.
The $\mathcal{H}(Z \mid Y)$ term, beyond potential overfitting implications, crucially links the representation to the data manifold's topology.

The generalization error of prognostic models is upper-bounded by $\mathcal{H}(Z \mid Y)$, formulated as
\begin{align}
    & \mathbb{E}_{\{\boldsymbol{x},\boldsymbol{z},y\}\thicksim \mathcal{S}}[||f(\boldsymbol{z})-{y}||_2] -\mathbb{E}_{\{\boldsymbol{x},\boldsymbol{z},y\}\thicksim \mathcal{T}}(||f(\boldsymbol{z})-{y}||_2) \notag \\
    & \leq 2KQ(\mathcal{H}(Z \mid Y)), \label{generalization_error}
\end{align}
% $||\cdot||_2$ is the $\ell_{2}$ norm
where $K$ is down-task regressor's Lipschitz constant, and $Q(\cdot)$ depends on $P(Z \mid Y)$, influencing the bound's tightness.
Furthermore, $\mathcal{H}(Z \mid Y)$ itself is bounded by the average IntDim of the conditional manifolds $\mathcal{M}_i\subset\mathcal{M}$ corresponding to observations $\boldsymbol{z}$ conditioned on $y = y_{i}$, formulated as
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{H}(Z \mid Y) \leq \mathbb{E}_{{y}_{i} \sim \mathcal{Y}}[-\log(\epsilon)IntDim(\mathcal{M}_i)+\log\frac{K^{\prime}}{C(\epsilon)}],
    \label{intrinsic_dimension} 
\end{equation}
where $\epsilon \to 0^{+}$ is a neighborhood radius, $K^{\prime}$ is a constant, and $C(\epsilon)$ is some function of $\epsilon$. 
% $i$ indexes samples
% $ID(\cdot)$ is the intrinsic dimension of the manifold
Conditions, proof methodologies and other details for these bounds \cite{Ghosh_Motani_2023, 10.5555/3692070.3694510} are satisfied by our model, and details are omitted for brevity.

Based on Eqs. \eqref{generalization_error}, \eqref{intrinsic_dimension}, and the IB principle's balance between compression and prediction, desirable degradation representations should possess two key geometric properties. These are: 
a low IntDim, reflecting a relatively simple manifold structure faithfully representable in lower dimensions, and continuous degradation patterns, represented as ordered and separable feature distributions for different aging states.
From a domain generalization perspective, these properties are vital. First, a low IntDim facilitates manifold contraction, simplifying the representation structure within which feature synthesis and distribution extrapolation are performed.
Second, continuity and order across degradation states, reflected in separable feature distributions, promote domain invariance and enhance feature manipulation by reducing inter-state interference and enabling reliable density estimation.

% Feature Engineering as Manifold Contraction
Acquiring topologically optimal degradation representation via IntDim regularization on raw signals is challenging due to costly per-step IntDim estimation and feature discontinuities from sparse coverage, impeding effective optimization. Consequently, inspired by PINN methods in battery research \cite{wang2024physics, 10251604}, we utilize a heuristic cycle signal reconstruction.
We implement this reconstruction by extracting statistical features from each sensor sequence of the original signal $\boldsymbol{x}_{t} \in \mathbb{R} ^ {L \times C}$. 
Representative examples include temporal differences or channel-wise moments such as mean and standard deviation. Concatenating these extracted statistics yields the reconstructed signal $\boldsymbol{\widetilde{x}}_{t} \in \mathbb{R} ^ {D \times 1}$, where $D$, the total number of extracted statistics, typically satisfies $C < D \ll L$. The precise input construction methodology is detailed in Section~\ref{subsec:experimental_setup}.
Empirically validated in Section~\ref{subsec:comparative_experiments}, this reconstruction mitigates temporal interference and expands features dimensionality to address insufficient coverage challenge, resulting in lower IntDim and more continuous degradation representation.

（2）结合流形收缩的 Mamba 模型

Selecting a backbone for mapping reconstructed signals to degradation features involves considering the task's sequential nature, linear time-invariant (LTI) system approximability for battery physics, and edge deployment constraints. Mamba's sequence modeling proficiency, LTI-compatible state-space mechanism, linear scaling, and parallel inference capabilities \cite{gu2024mamba} align well with these requirements, leading us to adopt it for this mapping task.

We form Mamba's input $\widetilde{X}_{Recon} \in \mathbb{R}^{D \times E}$ by vertically stack $E$ consecutive reconstructed signals.
This input is first embedded channel-independently into $\widetilde{X}_{Emb} \in \mathbb{R}^{H \times E}$, where $H$ is the hidden state dimension, then mapped via a three-branch residual structure into the feature space as
\begin{align}
& Z^{bypass}_{MRB} = SiLU(Linear(\widetilde{X}_{Emb})), \\
& Z^{main}_{MRB} = SSM(SiLU(Conv(Linear(\widetilde{X}_{Emb})))), \\
& Z_{MRB} = Linear(Z^{main}_{MRB} \odot Z^{bypass}_{MRB}) + \widetilde{X}_{Emb},
\end{align}
where $Z^{main}_{MRB}$, $Z^{bypass}_{MRB}$, and $Z_{MRB}$ are the main, bypass, and final Mamba residual block output. Operations include concatenation $\odot$, linear mapping $Linear(\cdot)$, convolution $Conv(\cdot)$, SiLU activation $SiLU(\cdot)$, and the S6 transformation $SSM(\cdot)$. The S6 model, an input-dependent discrete recurrent neural network, is formulated as
\begin{align}
& h_{t} = \overline{A}h_{t-1} + \overline{B}\widetilde{x}_{t}, \\
& \overline{A} = DR_{A}(\Delta, A) = exp(\Delta A), \\
& \overline{B} = DR_{B}(\Delta, A, B) = (\Delta A)^{-1}(exp(\Delta A)-I)\cdot\Delta B,
\end{align}
where $A$ and $B$ are trainable matrices. Adopting ZOH discretization $DR$ \cite{gu2024mamba}, $A$ is HiPPO-initialized \cite{20212610553629}, while $B$ and the discretization factor $\Delta$ are functions of input $\widetilde{x}_{t}$.

\subsection{不确定性驱动的退化特征合成策略}\label{subsec:UDFS}

To mitigate the issue of domain shift-induced statistics uncertainty and enhance model generalization capability, we propose UDFS method, which extrapolates distribution boundaries based on the contracted degradation representation.
First, we model feature statistics probabilistically via multivariate Gaussian distributions.
Second, we quantify statistics uncertainty by non-parametrically estimating the variance of these statistics.
Lastly, based on this uncertainty quantification, we synthesize features representing potential OOD degradation patterns through sampling, and the UDFS mechanism is subsequently integrated into the MMC model to enhance robustness. % against OOD instances.

（1）面向域漂移的多元高斯建模

Domain-specific degradation patterns are reflected in statistical distributions of features. We posit that domain shifts induce quantifiable inconsistencies in these statistics across different operational environments. Explicitly modeling this statistical inconsistency, particularly through the uncertainty inherent in feature statistics, enables the synthesis of features representative of unseen target domains from source data. This approach allows training degradation models with enhanced robustness.
Our method quantifies inter-domain variability by identifying the underlying distribution that governs feature statistics and by leveraging its sampling variance.
% 
Quantifying statistics uncertainty for robust degradation modeling essentially involves characterizing the low-order moments of feature statistic distributions.
This requires specific properties from the underlying distribution.
Ideally, it should satisfy Carleman's condition \cite{akhiezer2020classical}, ensuring unique determination by its moments, while its low-order moments should capture suffici distributional information. The multivariate Gaussian distribution, which satisfies Carleman's condition and is completely defined by its mean and covariance, fulfills these requirements. Given this theoretical alignment and its established use in battery degradation analyses, we employ a multivariate Gaussian framework to model feature statistics.

Specifically, for features $ Z_{MRB} \in \mathbb{R}^{B \times H \times E} $ derived from a minibatch of $ B $ samples, we employ batch-wise mean $ \mu_{Z} $ and standard deviation $ \sigma_{Z} $ as their statistical representations.
As described in the previous paragraph, we have $ \mu_{Z} \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu, \Sigma_{\mu}^{2}) $ and $ \sigma_{Z} \sim \mathcal{N}(\sigma, \Sigma_{\sigma}^{2}) $, where $ \mu $ and $ \sigma $ are the means of the feature statistics $ \mu_{Z} $ and $ \sigma_{Z} $, respectively.
We compute these statistics directly using the mean and standard deviation of features, formulated as
\begin{align}
    & \mu=\frac{1}{HE}\sum_{H}\sum_{E}Z_{MRB}, \\
    & \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{1}{HE}\sum_{H}\sum_{E}(Z_{MRB} - \mu)^{2}}.
\end{align}
And $ \Sigma_{\mu}^{2} $ and $ \Sigma_{\sigma}^{2} $ are the variances associated with feature statistics $\mu_{Z}$ and $\sigma_{Z}$, which also serve to model the statistics uncertainty induced by domain shifts.
These variances are estimated non-parametrically based on the observed intra-batch variability of the feature statistics, formulated as
\begin{align}
    & \Sigma_{\mu}^{2}=\frac{1}{B}\sum_{b\in B}(\mu-\mathbb{E}_{b}(\mu))^{2}, \\
    & \Sigma_{\sigma}^{2} = \frac{1}{B}\sum_{b\in B}(\sigma-\mathbb{E}_{b}(\sigma))^{2},
\end{align}
where $ \mathbb{E}_{b}(\mu) $ and $ \mathbb{E}_{b}(\sigma) $ are the expectations of mean and standard deviation of the $ b $-th sample within a minibatch.

（2）不确定性驱动的特征合成

Differentiable generation of feature statistics employs the reparameterization method under the assumption of Gaussian distributed original statistics with parameters $\mu$, $\Sigma_{\mu}$ and $\sigma$, $\Sigma_{\sigma}$, formulated as
\begin{align}
    & \mu_{Z}^{\prime}=\epsilon_{\mu}\Sigma_{\mu}+\mu,  \\
    & \sigma_{Z}^{\prime}=\epsilon_{\sigma}\Sigma_{\sigma}+\sigma, 
\end{align}
where $\mu_{Z}^{\prime}$ and $\sigma_{Z}^{\prime}$ are the generated feature mean and standard deviation, and $\epsilon_{\mu}$ and $\epsilon_{\sigma}$ are samples drawn from a standard normal distribution $\mathcal{N}(0, 1)$.
% 
The proposed UDFS utilizes the mean statistic $\mu$ as an anchor and standard deviation $\Sigma_{\mu}$ and $\Sigma_{\sigma}$ to probabilistically parameterize a offset, conferring adaptability to stochastic domain shifts through its variable direction and magnitude. Generating new statistics via reparameterization introduces controlled randomness, which augments the feature manifold surrounding the anchor statistics. This expansion improves coverage of potential unseen degradation patterns, enhancing model robustness and enabling generalization to OOD scenarios.
% 
Functionally, UDFS is integrated into the Mamba with manifold contraction model, operating between the encoder and the task-specific regression head for feature synthesis, formulated as
\begin{equation}
    G(Z_{MRB}) = \sigma_{Z}^{\prime}\left(\frac{Z_{MRB} - \mu}{\sigma}\right) + \mu_{Z}^{\prime}.
    \label{UDFS}
\end{equation}
This UDFS integration adds negligible constant-level computational overhead during training.
Furthermore, a dynamic activation mechanism based on Bernoulli sampling regulates its use, ensuring sufficient learning from real samples. At each training iteration, a Bernoulli value $p^{\prime}$ is drawn, and feature synthesis via UDFS proceeds only when $p^{\prime} < p_{0}$, where $p_{0}$ is a predefined activation threshold.

\subsection{感知长尾分布的自适应重平衡微调策略}\label{susbec:LARR}

To address the challenge of multi-source domain long-tail degradation distributions, we propose LARR, which employs feature distribution calibration specifically for enhancing battery degradation trajectory tracking and precise SOH estimation.
First, we employ feature distribution calibration to strengthen the correspondence between degradation feature similarity and degradation label proximity.
Second, we integrate this feature calibration strategy into UDFS to form LARR to eliminate domain-specific information tied to long-tail distributions, thereby purifying feature domain invariance.
Finally, we examine LARR's training dynamics and subsequently incorporate a head-bias mitigation strategy designed to improve degradation pattern generalization.

（1）基于特征核的特征统计校准

We discretize the bounded SOH label space via uniform binning into $\mathcal{B}$ intervals, indexed $\textit{b} = 1, 2, \dots, \mathcal{B}$. Battery degradation trajectories exhibit a long-tail distribution, characterized by substantial, often exponential, variation in the number of samples associated with each bin $\textit{b}$.
% 
Continuous degradation labels imply feature similarity relates inversely to label distance, unlike discrete labels. 
% 
The inherent long-tail distribution of degradation data violates this, as feature scarcity in tail bins leads to poor representations spuriously similar to head bin features.
% 
To counteract this, we calibrate features within each bin $\textit{b}$ by propagating information from adjacent bins via symmetric kernel functions. These corrected features, combined with the original observed features from the encoder-UDFS pipeline, are subsequently transformed to achieve a more balanced degradation feature representation.

We compute bin-wise feature statistics similarly to the batch-wise counterparts described in Section \ref{subsec:UDFS}. For features $Z_{MRB}$ within bin $\textit{b}$, the mean $\mu_{\textit{b}}$ and standard deviation $\sigma_{\textit{b}}$ are formulated as
\begin{align}
    & \mu_{\textit{b}} = \frac{1}{HE}\sum_{H}\sum_{E}Z_{MRB}, \\
    & \sigma_{\textit{b}} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{HE}\sum_{H}\sum_{E}(Z_{MRB} - \mu)^{2}}.
\end{align}
% 
To calibrate the feature distribution, we employ kernel smoothing across bins based on their corresponding SOH values. Without loss of generality, we select the Gaussian kernel for this purpose. This yields calibration statistics $\widetilde{\mu}_{\textit{b}}$ and $\widetilde{\sigma}_{\textit{b}}$, formulated as
% \begin{align}
%     \widetilde{\mu}_{\textit{b}} & = \sum_{1 \leq \textit{b}, \textit{b}^{\prime} \leq \mathcal{B}}Gau(y_{\textit{b}}, y_{\textit{b}^{\prime}})\mu_{\textit{b}^{\prime}} \notag \\
%     & = \sum_{1 \leq \textit{b}, \textit{b}^{\prime} \leq \mathcal{B}}exp(-\gamma\|y_{\textit{b}} - y_{\textit{b}^{\prime}}\|_{2})\mu_{\textit{b}^{\prime}},\label{mu_calibration} \\
%     \widetilde{\sigma}_{\textit{b}} & = \sum_{1 \leq \textit{b}, \textit{b}^{\prime} \leq \mathcal{B}}Gau(y_{\textit{b}}, y_{\textit{b}^{\prime}})\sigma_{\textit{b}^{\prime}} \notag \\
%     & = \sum_{1 \leq \textit{b}, \textit{b}^{\prime} \leq \mathcal{B}}exp(-\gamma\|y_{\textit{b}} - y_{\textit{b}^{\prime}}\|_{2})\sigma_{\textit{b}^{\prime}},\label{sigma_calibration}
% \end{align}
\begin{align}
    \widetilde{\mu}_{\textit{b}} = \sum_{1 \leq \textit{b}, \textit{b}^{\prime} \leq \mathcal{B}}exp(-\gamma\|y_{\textit{b}} - y_{\textit{b}^{\prime}}\|_{2})\mu_{\textit{b}^{\prime}},\label{mu_calibration} \\
    \widetilde{\sigma}_{\textit{b}} = \sum_{1 \leq \textit{b}, \textit{b}^{\prime} \leq \mathcal{B}}exp(-\gamma\|y_{\textit{b}} - y_{\textit{b}^{\prime}}\|_{2})\sigma_{\textit{b}^{\prime}},\label{sigma_calibration}
\end{align}
where $Gau(\cdot, \cdot)$ is the Gaussian kernel function with coefficient $\gamma$, and $y_{\textit{b}}$ and $y_{\textit{b}^{\prime}}$ are SOH values corresponding to the $\textit{b}$-th and $\textit{b}^{\prime}$-th bins, respectively.

（2）消除不平衡偏差的退化泛化

To address the long-tail distribution challenge, which persists in a consistent form both preceding and following the feature synthesis via $G(Z_{MRB})$, we implement the proposed LARR algorithm for head-bias-free correction of features.
LRAA performs feature distribution calibration utilizing two sets of bin-specific statistics, namely observed statistics $\{{\mu}_{\textit{b}}, {\sigma}_{\textit{b}}\}$ and calibration statistics $\{\widetilde{\mu}_{\textit{b}}, \widetilde{\sigma}_{\textit{b}}\}$, formulated as
\begin{equation}
    \widetilde{G}(Z_{MRB})=\widetilde{\sigma}_{\textit{b}}\left(\frac{G(Z_{MRB}) - \mu_{\textit{b}}}{\sigma_{\textit{b}}}\right)+\widetilde{\mu}_{\textit{b}}.
\end{equation}
Structurally identical to Eqs. \ref{UDFS}, the LARR approach, similar to UDFS, is applied exclusively during the training stage. % At iteration , the adjustment is performed via:
To facilitate clear exposition of the training dynamics, we denote the iteration-specific observed statistics and calibration statistics for bin $\textit{b}$ as $\{\mu_{\textit{b}}^{(i)}, \sigma_{\textit{b}}^{(i)}\}$ and $\{\widetilde{\mu}_{\textit{b}}^{(i)}, {\widetilde{\sigma}_{\textit{b}}^{(i)}}\}$, respectively, where $i$ indexes the training iteration. Within each training iteration, the degradation representation is adjusted, and subsequently, the corresponding calibration statistics are updated at the iteration's conclusion.
To ensure training stability, the observed statistics are updated via a momentum-based mechanism that integrates their values from the previous iteration and the static statistics, formulated as
\begin{align}
    & \mu_{\textit{b}}^{(i)} \leftarrow \alpha\mu_{\textit{b}}^{(i-1)}+(1-\alpha)\mu_{\textit{b}}, \\
    & {\sigma_{\textit{b}}}^{(i)} \leftarrow \alpha{\sigma_{\textit{b}}}^{(i-1)}+(1-\alpha)\sigma_{\textit{b}},
\end{align}
where $\alpha$ is the update momentum. The calibration statistics are updated according to Eqs. \eqref{mu_calibration} and \eqref{sigma_calibration}.

(2) pretrain-finetune-based methods, including CRNN \cite{D2EE01676A} and PINN+ \cite{wang2024physics};
(3) domain adaptation-based methods, including ConvMMD \cite{9788003}, LSTM-DA \cite{HAN2022230823}, DTKL \cite{10029904}, DDAN \cite{9560040}, and DR-Net \cite{WANG2023108897};
(4) domain generalization-based methods, including MAGNet \cite{TAN2024103725}, iTransformer \cite{liu2024itransformer}, PatchTST \cite{nie2023a}, and TSMixer \cite{chen2023tsmixer};
(5) target-trained RODTNet and RODTNet-LT, referred to as RODTNet+ and RODTNet-LT+, respectively.

We also examine the fine-tuned variants of general-purpose series modeling methods, denoted as iTransformer+, PatchTST+, and TSMixer+.

We conduct all the experiments on a server equipped with an Intel Core i9-13900KF CPU and an NVIDIA 4090 GPU (PyTorch 2.3.1 with CUDA 11.8).
All cycling data undergo min-max normalization to the range between 0 and 1. SOH labels follow dataset-specific scaling protocols, where the MATR dataset utilizes definition-based [0.8-1] scaling while other datasets adopted min-max [0, 1] normalization. RUL labels are normalized during training and denormalized during evaluation.
% 
% Details of domain splits are summarized in Table~\ref{tab:tasks}.
% 
Domain generalization-based methods follow a rigorous validation protocol \cite{9847099}, where validation sets are constructed exclusively from source-domain batteries with training-to-validation ratios of 3:1 (MATR batches 1–3, Tongji, XJTU), 4:1 (MATR batch 9), and 6:1 (HUST). All target-domain batteries are reserved for testing. 
% 
To facilitate domain alignment in domain adaptation-based methods, the target domain is further divided into a test subset, which includes batteries designated for degradation modeling, and a training subset comprising the remaining batteries. Both subsets ensure that the labels of degradation samples in the target domain remain completely inaccessible in model training phases.
% 
Models following the pretraining-finetuning paradigm mirror domain generalization protocols during pretraining, utilize identical test sets as domain adaptation methods, and further partition remaining target-domain batteries into 9:1 training-validation splits for finetuning.
% 
Details of sample construction, model architecture, and hyperparameters are provided in Table~\ref{tab:hyperparameters_setting}. The MSE loss function in conjunction with the Adam optimizer are used for model parameters optimization. The training process is conducted for a maximum of 100 epochs, with early stopping implemented.

We employ four metrics, namely Mean Absolute Error (MAE), Mean Square Error (MSE), Root Mean Square Error (RMSE), and Coefficient of Determination ($R^{2}$), to evaluate the model's generalization capability for OOD degradation patterns. Specifically, lower values of MAE, MSE, and RMSE signify improved generalization performance, whereas higher values of $R^{2}$ indicate stronger generalization ability.

Quantitatively, in OOD RUL prediction tasks, RODTNet reduces prediction errors by 40.76\% MAE and 28.65\% MSE compared to suboptimal baselines. Similarly, in OOD SOH estimation tasks, RODTNet-LT achieves 5.03\% MAE and 22.22\% MSE improvements over existing benchmarks. These average percentage improvements are calculated by comparing our method's performance against the second-best baseline when it is optimal for a given task, or against the best-performing baseline otherwise (with negative values denoting underperformance relative to the best), and then averaging these relative differences across all 11 generalization tasks. These enhancements validate the proposed method's robustness against domain shifts induced by heterogeneous degradation mechanisms.

\subsection{消融实验}\label{subsec:RODTNet_ablation_study}

We conduct ablation studies to evaluate the effectiveness of each components in RODTNet and RODTNet-LT. Table~\ref{tab:ablation_1} reports the MAE and MSE results for RUL prediction using RODTNet variants across 4 datasets. Correspondingly, Table~\ref{tab:ablation_study_2} presents these metrics for the SOH estimation task using both RODTNet and RODTNet-LT on the same datasets.

（）{Baseline}

We establish the baseline model using a fully linear backbone with downsampled raw cyclic signals as input.

（）{DGBL}

We obtain the DGBL model by adding UDFS strategy to the baseline. We observe an improvement in generalization performance, which proves the effectiveness of our proposed UDFS in expanding the distribution boundary and fitting OOD degradation patterns.

（）{LMCRDR}

We create the LMCRDR model by replacing the baseline's raw input with reconstructed input using the heuristic manifold contraction strategy. We observe an improvement in performance, demonstrating the good adaptability of our manifold contraction approach to the backbone and its superiority in generalizing degradation patterns.

（）{MCRDR}

We then develop the MCRDR model by replacing the linear backbone of LMCRDR with Mamba. We observed a further improvement in prognostic accuracy, indicating that Mamba effectively extracts domain-invariant features.

（）{LRODTNet}

We combine LMCRDR with the UDFS while retaining a linear backbone to form LRODTNet. We observe that the addition of UDFS improved performance over LMCRDR, thus proving that our propose feature synthesis strategy can extend the boundary of learned degradation representations possessing low intrinsic dimensions.

（）{RODTNet-LT}

For the task of SOH estimation involving long-tailed distributions, we obtained the RODTNet-LT variant by the adding LARR strategy. We observe that RODTNet-LT achieved lower generalization error compared to RODTNet, proving the effectiveness of LARR in removing head bias.

We conduct a sensitivity analysis across 4 datasets to evaluate the model's performance sensitivity to variations in batch size, encoder hidden dimension, number of encoder layers, and the UDFS activation threshold, using target domain RMSE as the evaluation metric.

（）{Batch Size}

We investigate batch size's impact on performance, testing \{32, 64, 128, 256, 512\}. Fig.~\ref{fig:sensitivity}(a) demonstrates that a size of 32 typically yields optimal performance in general scenarios. However, on MATR tasks, larger batches decrease error, suggesting sufficient samples allow approximation of domain shift, whereas smaller batches better capture degradation patterns in other tasks.

（）{Encoder Hidden Dimension}

We assess how encoder hidden dimension affects generalization, testing \{16, 32, 64, 128, 256\}. Fig.~\ref{fig:sensitivity}(b) reveals improved performance on Tongji and HUST tasks with larger dimensions, reflecting scalability. Conversely, performance degrades on MATR and XJTU, suggesting potential overfitting from excessive model scale.

（）{Number of Encoder Layers}

We analyze the number of encoder layers' influence on model scale and generalization, testing \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5\}. Fig.~\ref{fig:sensitivity}(c) indicates effects generally consistent with hidden dimension, where increasing layers appear more susceptible to overfitting bottlenecks.

（）{UDFS Activation Threshold}

We evaluate sensitivity to the UDFS activation threshold, which affects the real/generated data ratio, testing \{0.0008, 0.004, 0.02, 0.1, 0.5\}. Fig.~\ref{fig:sensitivity}(d) shows generalization improves across all datasets as the threshold increases, highlighting the importance of real source domain data for modeling OOD patterns.

We observe RODTNet is generally insensitive to hyperparameters, except for the complex XJTU task. For consistent comparisons, we fix parameters across tasks using the frequently optimal batch size of 32, along with hidden dimension 16 and 1 encoder layer for computational efficiency. Notably, RODTNet maintains strong performance even with these efficiency-prioritized parameters. We select a UDFS threshold of 0.5, effectively balancing minimal generalization error with reduced training overhead.

\section{本章小结}\label{sec:RODTNet_conclusion}

In this paper, we have presented RODTNet, a novel method for robust OOD battery degradation modeling, along with its long-tail-optimized variant RODTNet-LT.
% 
First, we have proposed a manifold contraction-based robust degradation representation extraction strategy. It achieves pattern continuity and compresses the feature manifold by combining heuristic signal reconstruction with latent dynamics generated via the Mamba mechanism.
% 
Second, we have developed an uncertainty-driven feature synthesis approach to address domain shifts. This method correlates feature statistics uncertainty with domain variations using probabilistic modeling, steering the creation of domain-invariant features and diverse OOD examples.
% 
Finally, we have designed a long-tail-aware adaptive rebalancing refinement technique based on feature distribution smoothing. By exploiting feature-label correlations, it alleviates head-bias in the synthesized features, improving the handling of imbalanced trajectories.
% 
Comprehensive experimental results across 11 OOD generalization tasks using 4 LIB aging datasets demonstrate the significant advantages of the proposed RODTNet and RODTNet-LT over 14 baselines and their 20 variants, underscoring the applicability and effectiveness of our proposed method in real-world battery management systems.

% Despite advancements, real-world battery operation necessitates continuous adaptation as degradation dynamics evolve throughout the lifespan, posing demands beyond initial OOD robustness. Exploring temporal domain adaptation thus presents a promising future direction, aiming to equip models with dynamic online update capabilities for sustained prognostic performance over time.
